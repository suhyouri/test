Tricks & Tracks, 2025,

5 × 5,5 × 2,5m
Wood, cardboard, electronics, motors
7:11 min video.

From ruses to rails, from clever tricks to fleeting images.

Once forbidden, "photophony"—the art of photographing with a phone—has become part of the museum landscape. Screens multiply, selfies fill the space, and artworks circulate long before they are truly seen.
With Tricks & Tracks, we embrace this paradox. The installation is designed for the phone itself: it is the visitor, the explorer, the storyteller. What you discover is a backstage world of makeshift illusions, miniature theaters, and playful devices. Against the tide of AI-generated images, we reclaim the magic of the physical: the ingenuity of tricks, their fragile materiality, and the earliest enchantments of theater and cinema.
Commissioned by Le Bel Ordinaire Pau.

Panneau Panache, 2025

Woods, ropes, pulleys
(4×4×2m)
Through a pulley system echoing the visual effects of early stagecraft, a dozen colorful shapes—suspended as though sprouting from the greenery—spring magically to life. With each gesture of the passerby they intertwine or overlap, the walker becoming the puppeteer of fleeting scenes revealed to all onlookers.
